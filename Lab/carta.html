Exotic donce moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work. I did study exotic tribal danes. To answer your question,
my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance
A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around
in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock or be fruitful.
Icelandic brownian shuffle
Before the Icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dancem whitch
involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffing their bodies around in imperceptably
tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired
modern styles such as Twerking.
Arctic robot dance
An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 19960s believed to have
discovered a new dance style characterised by robotic , stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitansts
of Northen Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this
because they were really cold.
